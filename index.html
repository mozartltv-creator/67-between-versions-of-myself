Why Do I Feel Like I’m Between Versions of Myself?

Core Thesis

You may feel like you are between versions of yourself when your previous identity no longer fits but a new one has not fully formed. If you feel like you are between versions of yourself, your identity may be restructuring rather than collapsing. The discomfort often reflects transition rather than regression.

1. When the Old Identity Fades

You may think, “I’m not who I used to be.” Attachment to former goals or roles may weaken gradually.

2. When the New Identity Is Not Yet Visible

Future direction may feel undefined. Values may be revising quietly.

3. The Gap Between Stability and Transformation

Identity transition can occur without dramatic events. Internal rearrangement may happen quietly.

4. Why It Feels Uncomfortable

Identity provides continuity. When continuity weakens, narrative coherence thins.

5. Common Signs You Feel Between Versions

  • You feel disconnected from your past self.
  • You hesitate to define long-term plans.
  • At times you wonder, “I don’t know who I am anymore.”
  • You sense change but cannot articulate it.
  • You feel suspended between phases.

6. How This State Develops

Transition may follow goal completion, burnout recovery, value shifts, or life stage changes. Identity adjusts gradually.

7. Where the Boundary Actually Lies

Feeling lost between identities does not automatically mean you have no identity. It may signal restructuring rather than failure.

Understanding this distinction separates transition from fragmentation.
